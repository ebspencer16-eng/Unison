How we think about this

Three principles behind everything we build.

Attune isn't built on generic advice. It's built on the conviction that real insight comes from your own answers — not someone else's assumptions.

01
Your answers, not ours

Attune doesn't tell you who you are as a couple. It helps you see yourselves — from your own responses, side by side. The insight is always yours.

02
Clarity before conflict

Assumptions about roles, finances, communication, and values quietly shape relationships long before they surface as friction. Naming them early changes everything.

03
Built for the long haul

Understanding isn't a one-time event. What each of you needs and envisions will evolve. Attune is designed to be revisited — not just a starting point, but an ongoing reference.

Grounded in research

This isn't intuition. It's evidence.

The exercises behind Attune are informed by decades of relationship science — not pop psychology. Here's what the research says about why structured understanding matters.

Expectations & satisfaction

Couples who discuss expectations early in the relationship report significantly higher relationship satisfaction — even years later. The conversations that feel premature are often the most valuable.

Gottman Institute — relationship research

Unspoken assumptions

67% of relationship dissatisfaction stems from unspoken assumptions about roles, finances, and communication styles. Most couples never surface these assumptions until they become problems.

Journal of Marriage and Family

Mutual understanding

Self-disclosure and partner understanding are among the strongest predictors of long-term relationship quality. Being genuinely known — and knowing your partner — is foundational, not supplementary.

Reis & Shaver — interpersonal process model
